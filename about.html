I’m from Oakville, Ontario, and my journalism journey spans nearly six years—though my love for storytelling has been lifelong. After earning a B.A. in Visual Culture & Communications at the University of Toronto and a postgraduate journalism certificate at Humber College, I dove head‑first into community reporting in Guelph. In just over three years there, I wrote more than 1,500 articles, learning what makes local communities tick and how to give everyday voices a platform. For the past three years I’ve chased tight daily deadlines on television and radio in Toronto and Nova Scotia, covering everything from breaking news to long‑form features. Quick turn‑arounds and high stakes energize me, but what keeps me hooked is impact—stories that leave audiences thinking, feeling, and acting. For me, the joy lies in uncovering extraordinary stories within the ordinary. — Anam Khan
